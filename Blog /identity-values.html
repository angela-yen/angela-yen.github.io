Ethical Decision

When I was teaching, there were many times where the students had “broken” school rules. In some cases it was just a small uniform problem, in others it was more severe issues. An example would be the students walking into the classroom smelling of cigarette smoke. In the beginning of my teaching journey I was very alarmed by this situation and would always report them to the deans. However, as I started to see the repercussions of the this, I found that there were not supportive structures in place for students. Rather, reporting the students to senior management would mean they would miss out on their learning and be punished by a system that was not created to serve many of them.

As a result of this, I have found instances where I ended up speaking to the students about what was going on for them and the reasons around their decision to smoke. To me, it was more important that I had a good relationship with them and they felt safe in my classroom to get support and then refer them to the health centre than for them to be stood down and punished.

The consequence of this decision was that other teachers with the same students would often come to me and ask why I didn’t tell the dean and the fact is, I did not see them smoking in my class and therefore, do not have solid evidence to suggest that they themselves were the ones smoking. At the end of the day, I was able to develop a trusting relationship with the students and for many of them, they were more open for me to have a conversation about the risk to their health than if they were punished for it.

Group Work

In the semester just passed, I had to work with my group to create a shared group report and presentations. It was a massive assignment and although we have an amazing group of people, it was very difficult to delegate tasks. One of the issues we came across was deadline. We had allocated tasks to each person however, for some of the tasks, they could only be done when the previous task was completed.

Communication was very important for us, we found that setting a deadline for each section of the task was more effective. This way each person can pass onto the next person effectively. We were also aware as a team that there may have been other deadlines or assignments that we had to do on the side, therefore, we create a safe space for people to reach out when they felt like they were struggling.

At the start of the project we also asked each person what skills they had and what types of tasks they enjoyed doing. The entire project had different sections including PowerPoints, writing and video recording. We were able to delegate tasks based on each group members’ preferences and they unique skills sets. At the end of the project we found that this was very effective as it meant that collaboratively we were able to create a high standard project as our unique skillsets were able to give a well-rounded outcome.

This was a massive learning experience and I found definitely would continue to use these skills again in the future projects. Other strategies I might try is using different platforms to work on, we were using google docs and slides, which were useful but I feel like there may be other platforms around that may be useful in the future.
