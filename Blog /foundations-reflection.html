Foundation Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

  1. I don’t mind asking for help and reaching out to people however, this is usually to a peer rather than to a facilitator.
  2. Putting a lot of pressure on myself can lead to lack of motivation to start the task.
  3. I work better with people rather than self-directed in isolation.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self awareness in learning and programming?

All of these roles help with problem solving and also interacting with other people, working in groups and teams. It can also help with self growth within the learning journey.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

That a lot of the things I knew about myself already were still relevant within coding and despite having awareness of short comings, it is still hard to overcome them. After talking to others as well, I found that many of the feelings I was having was similar within other people in programme as well.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Having motivation to do it after a long day/week of technical coding. I found that it was less quantifiable which meant the “progress” is harder to see.

Why do you think that we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

It helps with becoming not only a good programmer but also a good person. It teaches how to get past issues and also work with others. It also helps create a growth environment that accommodates for issues that could come up in a work environment.

• Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

It doesn’t feel like a waste of time however, I do know that I put less emphasis on it as I am more worried about not being able to learn how to code. I think they are both equally as important, if anything, becoming a smart learner and problem solving comes with the core teachings. As a result, I do see value in core however, I did find it difficult to have the same level of motivation towards it than compared to learning for example, JavaScript.
